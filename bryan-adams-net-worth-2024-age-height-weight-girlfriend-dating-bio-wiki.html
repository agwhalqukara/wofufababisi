Early Life

Bryan was born on 5 November 1959, to Elizabeth Jane and Captain Conrad J. Adams, in Kingston, Ontario, Canada. He went to Colonel By Secondary School in Eat Ottawa.

At 17, he became a part of a band called Sweeney Todd. Although they did enjoy some success, Adams left the band within a year.

He then signed on with A&M records in 1978.

While he was still exploring the music scene, he met guitarist Keith Richards and Jim Vallance, both of who are still a mark of his music-making process even today.

Age, Height, and Weight

Career

He released his first album in 1980. This was followed by You Want It You Got It, in 1981. He released his third album, Cuts Like a Knife, in 1983. His fourth album, Reckless, was released in 1984 and contained hit songs like, “Summer of ’69”, “Heaven”, “It’s Only Love”, “One Night Love Affair”, and “Run to You”. It is Bryan’s best-selling album and has been certified platinum five times in the USA.

From the last month of 1984, Adams decided to start his two-year-long world tour to promote his album. It was quite a successful tour with sold-out venues in two continents.

In 1985, he collaborated with Roger Daltrey and created two tracks with him.

Adams’s fifth album, Into the Fire which was released in 1987, contained the chart-topping songs, “Hearts on Fire” and “Heat of the Night”.

1991 saw his sixth album, Waking Up the Neighbours, which contained the hit songs, “(Everything I Do) I Do It for You”, “Do I Have to Say the Words?” and “Thought I’d Died and Gone to Heaven”. That same year he started his second world tour, “Waking Up the World”.

In 1993, he collaborated with Sting and Rod Stewart to create the hit song, “All for Love”.

1996 saw the release of yet another album called, 18 til I Die.

He released MTV Unplugged in 1997 and On a Day Like Today in 1998.

The Best of Me, was an album released by Adams, compiling all his greatest hits.

In 2002, he wrote and sung for the soundtrack of the film, Spirit: Stallion of the Cimarron.

In 2006, he sang “Never Let Go” for the film, The Guardian.

He also composed songs for Disney’s Bridge to Terabithia.

In 2008, he released his album titled, 11.

In 2014, he released a new album called, Tracks of My Years.

2015 saw the release of yet another album called, Get Up.

Awards & Achievement

Bryan Adams has received 20 Juno Awards and 56 nominations. He has been nominated for the Grammy Awards 15 times and has won it in 1992 for ‘Best Song Written Specifically for a Motion Picture or Television’.

He has also been nominated a grand total of five times for the Golden Globe Awards as well as thrice for the Academy Awards.

He has also been nominated multiple times for the ASCAP, MTV and American Music awards.
