Susan Wojcicki’s husband is the tech-savvy Dennis Troper. Like Wojcicki, Troper is also known for his career in the tech world.

Susan Wojcicki married Dennis Troper in Belmont, California, on August 23, 1998. They have five children.

Dennis Troper is a man who has accomplished a lot in his career. After graduating with a Master’s degree in Applied Economics from the University of California, Santa Cruz, Troper began his professional journey. 

He held various positions at Deloitte, Concur, and Orazle before joining Google in 2003 as a Group Project Manager.

At Google, he has been involved in successful product launches like AdWords, G Suite, and Google Fiber. According to his LinkedIn profile, he was appointed Director of Product Management- Trust, Privacy, and User Data. 

This shows his vast range of skills and ability to pursue meaningful projects that profoundly affect people worldwide.

Originally from Costa Rica, Troper entered the Bay Area more than 20 years ago, quickly becoming involved with local institutions such as Gideon Hausner Jewish Day School and Anti-Defamation League. 

He completed his undergraduate education at the University of Texas at Austin, majoring in Economics with a Master’s degree in Applied Economics from the University of California Santa Cruz.

Why is Susan Wojcicki leaving Youtube?

With nearly 25 years at Google under her belt, Susan Wojcicki recently announced her decision to leave YouTube as CEO on February 16th this year to focus on her family, health, and her projects while continuing to remain involved working with YouTube teams coaching team members, and meeting creators. 

Her successor will be Neil Mohan, who currently serves as Chief Product Officer for the company.

The contributions made by both these individuals demonstrate their commitment to innovation and development across multiple platforms while maintaining a socially conscious approach that supports those in need around them both locally as well as globally.
